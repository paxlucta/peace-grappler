6 Specific Recommendations

1
Stop posting at 21:50 unless it's breaking news
Consolidate to 13:00, 16:00, and 19:00 UTC slots. The 21:50 window is consistently burning content that deserves a better audience.
2
Build deliberate storytelling series around specific fighters
Kevin Vallejos's 3-part narrative averaged 25s watch time because each video creates tension the next resolves. Film 5–6 story questions per fighter and cut into a week-long arc. Caio Borralho, Manumito, and Bruno Lopes all have the depth for this.
3
Chase the spontaneous encounter
The Caio/Bronx video is unteachable as a format but the lesson is clear: be present with a camera at unscripted moments. When a fighter unexpectedly runs into a legend at weigh-ins or the octagon — drop everything. That content is worth more than 20 produced camp videos.
4
Plant the hook in the first line of every caption
Best captions open with a specific surprising claim: "Tiwi acredita no nocaute!", "Kevin Vallejos admite que pensou em desistir", "Caio se encontra com Charles do Bronx." Nobody clicks for "camp feito, foco total."
5
Manufacture a content arc for every Brazilian UFC card
For every Brazilian UFC fighter: camp access → prediction videos from teammates → fight week arrival → walkout → result. Treat it as a mini-series, not a single post. UFC 326 week proved this drives 3–5x normal performance.
6
Design clips to be reshared by fighters themselves
The 204-share video worked because someone with reach reshared it. Deliberately create short clips with the fighter's face front-and-center in the first frame, a quotable moment, and an impressive physical display — short enough to DM, compelling enough to post on their own story.
